Get Social!




Anxiety can stem from being lonely. Getting social and breaking out of your
shell can help you get more comfortable, because in the end, it’s all about
enjoying your life and being happy.

Social Media is a great way for people to start. While many see it as a
distraction or negative influence, there are countless wonderful things
you can do online. For friends that you can only see once in awhile, it’s
a way for you to stay connected.

Websites like Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn, Google+, Youtube, PinInterest,
Instagram, Tumblr, Flickr and Reddit are sweeping the nation.


Take a quiz on what social media would be right for you!

Talk to people!

Regret, guilt, embarrassment and fear cause extreme anxiety at times.
But it’s so important to realize that you’re not alone.

Interviews
When you realize that you’re being evaluated by someone else, it touches
on your pride, your future and your image. The anxiety to do well is
intimidating. People take it for more than it is and confuse another
person’s opinion to be truth. In reality, another person can never truly
understand you in a short amount of time or in a single page of resume.
Without living your life or being you, their opinion is just like what
it sounds; their opinion. Hold your head high and don’t let setbacks define
who you are or will be.

Fights

There are about four different kinds of fights: fights with people you love,
fights with people you hate, fights with yourself and fights with anyone else.
Whether the fights are physical or mental (or verbal), it’s important to stick
to what you believe in. It’s unrealistic to think that everyone will agree with
you and everyone should like you. There are people you’ll get along with at
one point and then drift apart. In the end, it’s about you. It’s about your
happiness and as long as you don’t doubt yourself and truly try to do what you
believe is right, then that’s honestly all that matters.



There are other people who you could talk too, such as professionals.
It doesn’t have to be in person either; there are where you could talk whether
it be online or on the phone. Also, there are a lot of accepting communities
that includes other people with the same struggles as you. They form
support groups and there could be one near you. These sites would
could you find these support groups and connect you with them.

Phone Numbers You Can Call:
The National Alliance on Mental Illness Mental Illness is nothing to feel bad about.
These people work trying to reform mental health and to build better
lives for those who are affected.
If you ever want to talk to anyone here, call 800-950-NAMI (6264).

The Samaritan - A Helping Hand They are a completely confidential hotline that you can call.
It is free, and immediately accessible 24/7.
It is a safe place to turn to in case you ever want to talk.
